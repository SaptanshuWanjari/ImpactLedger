                                    CHAPTER 3
                             LITERATURE REVIEW


Several web-based platforms currently exist to support NGO operations and
charitable fundraising. Prominent examples include GiveIndia, Milaap, Ketto, and
GlobalGiving, which primarily focus on crowdfunding and project listing. These
platforms allow organizations to create fundraising campaigns and receive
donations, but they offer limited support for detailed activity tracking, resource
utilization reporting, or outcome-based impact visualization. Their design prioritizes
fundraising over operational accountability.


In terms of technology, most modern NGO management portals are built using
JavaScript-based frontend frameworks such as React or Angular for user interaction,
paired with backend services using Node.js or Django for business logic. Databases
such as PostgreSQL, MongoDB, or Firebase Firestore are commonly used for data
storage. These technology stacks are capable of building scalable, responsive web
applications, but existing platforms have not fully leveraged their potential for
implementing comprehensive impact tracking pipelines.


A critical limitation of current solutions is the absence of real-time analytics and
structured reporting features. Many platforms display data in static formats that are
not easily interpretable by non-technical users, such as community volunteers or
grassroots donors. Furthermore, few platforms allow NGOs to document detailed
activity logs or provide audit trails for resource usage. The user interfaces are often
complex and not optimized for users with limited digital literacy.


ImpactLedger is designed to overcome these limitations by integrating real-time
data visualization, a clean and accessible user interface, and structured impact
reporting. Unlike existing platforms that focus on a single function such as
fundraising, ImpactLedger provides an end-to-end solution that supports the full
lifecycle of community project management, from inception and activity tracking to
outcome reporting and stakeholder communication.
