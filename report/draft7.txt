                                    ABSTRACT

ImpactLedger For NGO Funding Analysis is a web-based platform developed to
enhance transparency, accountability, and efficiency in community-driven
initiatives. Many non-governmental organizations (NGOs) and social projects
currently rely on manual or unstructured systems for managing donations,
tracking activities, and measuring impact. This often leads to issues such as lack of
visibility, inefficient reporting, and reduced trust among stakeholders.

The proposed system addresses these challenges by providing a centralized
platform where NGOs, donors, and volunteers can interact seamlessly.
ImpactLedger enables users to create and manage projects, record activities, track
donations, and monitor overall impact through a structured and organized system.
The platform also offers data visualization features such as dashboards, charts,
and progress indicators, making complex information easy to understand and
analyze.

Built using modern web technologies such as React/Next.js for the frontend,
Node.js for backend services, and MongoDB/Firebase for data storage, the system
ensures efficient performance, scalability, and secure data handling. Role-based
access control further enhances security by restricting system functionalities based
on user roles.

By providing real-time insights into project progress and resource utilization,
ImpactLedger helps build trust between stakeholders and supports better
decision-making. Overall, the platform serves as a reliable digital solution for
improving transparency and effectiveness in community engagement projects.

                                    CHAPTER 1
                                 INTRODUCTION


Community Engagement Projects (CEPs) serve as a bridge between academic
learning and real-world problem-solving, enabling students to design and deploy
technology-driven solutions for societal challenges. These projects provide an
opportunity to apply theoretical knowledge in practical settings while contributing
meaningfully to communities that often lack access to modern digital infrastructure.
ImpactLedger is one such initiative, developed in collaboration with Lions Club
Nagpur Sawaj to address a persistent gap in the operational transparency of non-
governmental organizations (NGOs).


In the current landscape, NGOs and community-driven projects rely heavily on
manual or informal methods for tracking activities, recording donations, and
reporting outcomes. This approach not only introduces the risk of human error and
data inconsistency, but also reduces the confidence of donors and other stakeholders
in the overall management of projects. The absence of structured digital systems
leads to delayed reporting, unverified claims of impact, and an overall decline in
accountability.


Digital solutions play a crucial role in modern community engagement projects by
enabling structured data management, real-time monitoring, and improved
transparency. Unlike traditional manual systems, digital platforms ensure accurate
record-keeping and reduce the risk of data loss or inconsistency. They also allow
stakeholders to access project information instantly, regardless of location. This
improves accountability, strengthens donor confidence, and enables better decision-
making through data-driven insights.


The primary objective of ImpactLedger is to develop a centralized, web-based
platform that enables NGOs to record and manage their activities, track incoming
contributions, and present verified impact data through interactive dashboards. By
digitizing these processes, the platform ensures that all stakeholders, including




                                                                                     11
donors, beneficiaries, and administrators, have access to accurate and up-to-date
information at all times.


The core problem addressed by this project is the lack of structured transparency
mechanisms in community initiatives, which results in mismanagement of
resources, weakened donor relationships, and inefficient impact reporting.
ImpactLedger aims to resolve this by providing a reliable digital ecosystem for
NGO operations.


The scope of the project covers the complete design and development of a web
application with features for user authentication, project creation, activity tracking,
donation recording, and data visualization. The system is intended for use by NGOs
registered on the platform and their associated stakeholders.

                                    CHAPTER 2
                            COMMUNITY ANALYSIS


The target community for ImpactLedger comprises four distinct groups: NGO
administrators, field volunteers, donors, and beneficiaries. NGO administrators are
responsible for planning and executing social initiatives, volunteers assist in ground-
level implementation, donors provide financial and material support, and
beneficiaries are the individuals or groups who directly receive services.
Understanding the specific roles and interactions of these groups was essential for
designing a platform that genuinely addresses their needs.


Interactions with Lions Club Nagpur Sawaj, along with informal surveys and
discussions with NGO coordinators and volunteers, revealed several operational
challenges. Most organizations maintain records using physical ledgers or
fragmented spreadsheets, which are prone to inconsistencies and difficult to audit.
There is no single system through which all stakeholders can view project progress
or verify the utilization of donated resources. This lack of a unified platform results
in delayed reporting, reduced accountability, and declining donor participation.


A web-based solution is well-suited to address these challenges. A digital platform
can automate routine record-keeping, provide centralized access to project data, and
offer real-time visibility into organizational activities. Unlike offline methods, a web
application is accessible from any device with an internet connection, making it
convenient for both urban and semi-urban users. It also enables systematic storage
and retrieval of historical data, which is valuable for long-term impact assessment.


The key stakeholders whose requirements shaped the design of ImpactLedger are
NGO administrators, who need efficient tools for project creation and reporting;
donors, who require transparent financial tracking and impact visibility; and
beneficiaries, who benefit indirectly through better-managed service delivery. The
system has been designed to serve each of these groups through a unified yet role-
aware interface.

                                    CHAPTER 3
                             LITERATURE REVIEW


Several web-based platforms currently exist to support NGO operations and
charitable fundraising. Prominent examples include GiveIndia, Milaap, Ketto, and
GlobalGiving, which primarily focus on crowdfunding and project listing. These
platforms allow organizations to create fundraising campaigns and receive
donations, but they offer limited support for detailed activity tracking, resource
utilization reporting, or outcome-based impact visualization. Their design prioritizes
fundraising over operational accountability.


In terms of technology, most modern NGO management portals are built using
JavaScript-based frontend frameworks such as React or Angular for user interaction,
paired with backend services using Node.js or Django for business logic. Databases
such as PostgreSQL, MongoDB, or Firebase Firestore are commonly used for data
storage. These technology stacks are capable of building scalable, responsive web
applications, but existing platforms have not fully leveraged their potential for
implementing comprehensive impact tracking pipelines.


A critical limitation of current solutions is the absence of real-time analytics and
structured reporting features. Many platforms display data in static formats that are
not easily interpretable by non-technical users, such as community volunteers or
grassroots donors. Furthermore, few platforms allow NGOs to document detailed
activity logs or provide audit trails for resource usage. The user interfaces are often
complex and not optimized for users with limited digital literacy.


ImpactLedger is designed to overcome these limitations by integrating real-time
data visualization, a clean and accessible user interface, and structured impact
reporting. Unlike existing platforms that focus on a single function such as
fundraising, ImpactLedger provides an end-to-end solution that supports the full
lifecycle of community project management, from inception and activity tracking to
outcome reporting and stakeholder communication.

impact verification.

                           SYSTEM REQUIREMENTS


The functional requirements of ImpactLedger define the specific operations the
system must support. The platform requires a secure user authentication system with
role-based access control, enabling NGO administrators, volunteers, and donors to
access features appropriate to their roles. The system must support the creation,
editing, and deletion of community projects, along with the ability to log individual
activities associated with each project. A donation recording module is required to
track contributions received, while an impact dashboard must display aggregated
metrics in a graphical format. The platform must also support export of reports for
offline reference.


Non-functional requirements address the quality attributes of the system. The
application must be responsive, ensuring usability across desktop, tablet, and mobile
devices. Security requirements include encrypted data transmission using HTTPS,
password hashing, and session management to prevent unauthorized access. The
platform must maintain consistent performance under concurrent user load, with
page response times not exceeding three seconds under typical conditions. The user
interface must be simple and accessible enough for non-technical users, adhering to
standard usability principles.


The hardware and software requirements for deploying and using the system are
minimal. End users require a device equipped with a modern web browser and a
stable internet connection. On the server side, a cloud hosting environment capable
of running a Node.js application is required, along with a MongoDB or Firebase
database instance for persistent data storage. Development tools include Visual
Studio Code, Git for version control, and Postman for API testing. The system is
designed to operate efficiently on standard infrastructure without the need for
specialized hardware.

                                SYSTEM DESIGN


ImpactLedger follows a three-tier client-server architecture. The presentation layer
consists of a React or Next.js-based frontend application that handles all user
interactions and renders data visually. The application layer is a Node.js backend
server that processes requests, enforces business rules, and manages communication
with the database. The data layer uses MongoDB or Firebase for persistent storage
of project records, user profiles, activity logs, and contribution data. Communication
between the frontend and backend is handled through RESTful API endpoints,
ensuring a clean separation of concerns and facilitating independent development
and testing of each tier.


The database design is structured around the core entities of the system. The
primary collections include Users, Projects, Activities, Donations, and
ImpactMetrics. Each project document contains references to associated activities
and donations, allowing for efficient querying of project-level data. The Users
collection stores role-based information, enabling the backend to enforce access
permissions at the API level.


The system design includes standard UML representations to model its behavior.
The use-case diagram captures the interactions between NGO administrators,
donors, and the system, covering actions such as project creation, activity logging,
donation recording, and report generation. Sequence diagrams describe the flow of
data during key operations such as user authentication and dashboard data retrieval,
illustrating how requests pass from the frontend through the backend to the database
and back.


The user interface is designed with clarity and accessibility in mind. The dashboard
presents key impact metrics using charts and summary cards, giving administrators
a quick overview of project status. Data entry forms are structured and validated to
prevent incorrect submissions. Navigation is kept simple and consistent across all
pages to minimize the learning curve for new users.

                                 METHODOLOGY


The development of ImpactLedger follows an Agile methodology, organized into
iterative sprints. Each sprint involves planning, development, testing, and review
phases, allowing the team to incorporate feedback progressively and adapt to
changing requirements. This approach is well-suited for a community-engaged
project where user needs evolve through ongoing interaction with the partner
organization, Lions Club Nagpur Sawaj. Regular review sessions with NGO
coordinators helped refine features and prioritize development tasks.


The technology stack was selected based on criteria of community adoption,
documentation availability, and suitability for the project's requirements. The
frontend is developed using React with Next.js to enable server-side rendering,
improving page load performance and search engine visibility. The backend is
implemented in Node.js with the Express framework, providing a lightweight and
efficient server environment for handling API requests. MongoDB is used as the
primary database for its document-oriented structure, which aligns well with the
hierarchical nature of project and activity data. Firebase is considered as an
alternative for its real-time synchronization capabilities.


Data collection for understanding user needs was carried out through structured
surveys distributed to NGO volunteers and administrators, as well as informal
interviews with Lions Club Nagpur Sawaj members. The surveys covered topics
such as current record-keeping practices, pain points in project management, and
expectations from a digital system. The collected responses informed the
prioritization of features and the design of the user interface, ensuring that the
platform reflects actual operational requirements rather than assumptions.

                               IMPLEMENTATION


The implementation of ImpactLedger is organized into three primary modules:
frontend, backend, and database integration. The frontend module, developed using
React and Next.js, includes the following key components: a login and registration
interface with form validation, a project management dashboard for creating and
editing NGO initiatives, an activity logging interface for recording field-level
actions, a donation tracker with input forms and summary views, and an impact
visualization panel featuring bar charts, line graphs, and summary statistics rendered
using a JavaScript charting library.


The backend module is implemented in Node.js using the Express framework. It
exposes a set of RESTful API endpoints that the frontend consumes for all data
operations. Key endpoints include routes for user authentication using JSON Web
Tokens (JWT), project CRUD operations, activity and donation recording, and
retrieval of aggregated impact data for dashboard rendering. Input validation and
error handling are implemented at the API layer to ensure data integrity.
Middleware functions enforce role-based access control, preventing unauthorized
users from accessing or modifying restricted resources.


The database module uses MongoDB with a structured collection schema. Data
relationships are managed through document references, and indexes are applied on
frequently queried fields such as project identifiers and user roles to optimize read
performance. The integration between the backend and database is handled through
Mongoose, an object data modeling library that provides schema validation and
query abstraction.

the source code is available at https://github.com/SaptanshuWanjari/ImpactLedger.

                             RESULTS AND IMPACT


The ImpactLedger platform demonstrates satisfactory performance across its core
functional areas. API response times for standard data retrieval operations were
observed to be within acceptable limits during testing, and the application
maintained stability under concurrent access by multiple test users. The frontend
renders correctly across desktop and mobile browsers, confirming the
responsiveness of the interface. Data submitted through forms is accurately stored
and retrieved, and the dashboard visualizations update in real time as new records
are added.


Feedback gathered from volunteers and administrators associated with Lions Club
Nagpur Sawaj indicated a positive reception of the platform. Users reported that the
project dashboard made it easier to understand the status of ongoing initiatives at a
glance, and that the activity logging feature simplified the process of maintaining
records. Donor-facing views were appreciated for providing a clear summary of
contributions and their associated outcomes, increasing confidence in the platform's
transparency.


Prior to the implementation of ImpactLedger, the partner organization managed

project activities.

                                    CHAPTER 9
                                    DISCUSSION


The development of ImpactLedger presented several technical and organizational
challenges. On the technical side, managing the relationship between dynamic
project data and real-time dashboard rendering required careful API design to avoid
unnecessary database queries and excessive client-side processing. Ensuring
consistent state management across multiple components in the React frontend was
also a significant concern, particularly when multiple users interact with the same
project data simultaneously. Integrating secure authentication using JSON Web
Tokens while maintaining a smooth user experience required iterative refinement of
the login and session management logic.


The system has certain limitations that reflect the constraints of the current
implementation phase. The platform has been tested primarily with a limited
dataset, and its behaviour under large-scale usage with thousands of concurrent
users has not been fully evaluated. Additionally, the current version does not include
an advanced notification system to alert stakeholders of new activities or donations
in real time. The absence of a mobile application also limits accessibility for users in
low-connectivity environments who prefer native apps over browser-based
interfaces.


From a project management perspective, coordinating the development timeline
with academic commitments and partner organization schedules required flexibility
and regular communication. Not all originally planned features could be fully
implemented within the available time, and some aspects of the user interface, such
as multi-language support, were deferred to a future phase of development.


The project provided valuable learning experiences in full-stack web development,
Agile project management, and stakeholder communication. It reinforced the
importance of conducting thorough requirement analysis before development and of
maintaining iterative feedback loops with end users to ensure that the delivered
product is aligned with real-world needs.

                                    CHAPTER 10
                      CONCLUSION AND FUTURE SCOPE


ImpactLedger successfully achieves its primary objective of providing a transparent
and accountable digital platform for managing community-driven NGO initiatives.
The system addresses a genuine operational need identified through engagement
with Lions Club Nagpur Sawaj and enables organizations to replace manual, error-
prone record-keeping with a structured, accessible web application. The platform
improves visibility into project activities for all stakeholders, reduces the time
required for report preparation, and strengthens donor confidence through verifiable
impact data.


The project demonstrates that student-led community engagement initiatives can
deliver practical and meaningful technological contributions when guided by a clear
understanding of real-world problems. Through the application of modern web
development practices and an Agile development approach, the team was able to
build and deploy a functional platform within an academic semester.


Several enhancements are planned for future development cycles. The most
immediate priority is the development of a mobile application for Android and iOS
to extend accessibility to users who primarily use smartphones. Integration of AI-
based analytics is envisioned to enable predictive insights, such as estimating the
projected impact of a project based on historical data patterns. Blockchain
technology is also being considered as a mechanism for creating tamper-proof
records of donations and activity logs, further strengthening the platform's
transparency guarantees. Additional improvements to the dashboard, including
customizable report templates and export capabilities in multiple formats, will
enhance usability for diverse organizational needs.
